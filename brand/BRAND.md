# Malon Labs Ltd — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Company Name:** Malon Labs Ltd
**Consulting Arm:** Malon Labs Consulting
**Tagline:** "Smarter Operations. Stronger Business."
**Alternative taglines:**

- "We build, manage, and optimize technology for your business."
- "Technology That Works For Your Business"

## Logo

The Malon Labs logo features a molecular/network structure forming the letter "M", representing connection, innovation, and systematic thinking. The design uses clean geometric lines with node points, conveying both scientific precision and interconnectedness.

### Logo Files (in `brand/logo/`)

| File | Variant | Use On |
| ------ | --------- | -------- |
| `malon-logo-dark-bg.jpg` | Dark navy background, white icon | Light page backgrounds — the navy square sits on white |
| `malon-logo-light-bg.jpg` | White/light background, navy icon | Dark sections or as inline logo |
| `malon-logo-primary.jpeg` | Primary (dark bg) | Default / hero usage |

### Consulting Sub-brand

For consulting-specific materials, use the standard logo with "CONSULTING" added below "malonlabs" in a lighter weight or in the emerald green accent color. Do not create a separate logo.

Format: `malonlabs` (existing logo) + `CONSULTING` (subtitle in Poppins Light, #1A8F6E)

### Logo Usage Rules

- Minimum clear space: height of the "m" in "malonlabs" on all sides
- Never stretch, rotate, or apply effects
- On dark backgrounds: use the light-bg variant (navy icon on white)
- On light backgrounds: use the dark-bg variant (white icon on navy)
- Minimum display size: 80px wide for digital, 20mm for print

- These colors work well in both digital and print

### Font Rules — Critical

- **"malonlabs" / "Malon Labs" must ALWAYS use the Viga font** — on the website, presentations, proposals, pitch decks, and any branded material. No exceptions.
- Load Viga from Google Fonts: `https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Viga&display=swap`

- If Viga is unavailable (e.g., plain text email), use the closest fallback: Arial Black or Impact.

## Tone of Voice

### Do

- Speak in outcomes and business results ("reduce costs by 40%")
- Use simple, clear language a non-technical CEO would understand
- Reference real Tanzanian business contexts and examples
- Be confident but not arrogant
- Use "we" language — position as a partner, not a vendor
- Highlight the breadth of services (we build, manage, AND consult)

### Don't

- Use technical jargon in client-facing materials (no "LLMs", "Kubernetes clusters", "CI/CD pipelines")
- Be vague ("improve things" → instead: "cut report generation time from 3 days to 3 hours")
- Sound like a Silicon Valley startup — ground everything in East African business reality
- Over-promise — be specific about what technology can and can't do
- Use stock phrases like "leverage synergies" or "paradigm shift"
- Treat the three services as separate companies — they are integrated pillars

### Example Phrases

- ✅ "Your finance team spends 20 hours a month on reports. We'll get that down to 4."
- ✅ "We built an app for a logistics company in Dar that handles 2,000 deliveries daily."
- ✅ "Your network went down 6 times last quarter. With our monitoring, that becomes zero."
- ❌ "Our cutting-edge solutions leverage machine learning to optimize workflows."

- ❌ "We implement state-of-the-art cloud-native microservices architectures."

## Website Design Direction

### General Aesthetic

- Clean, modern, navy-dominant with strategic use of white space
- The molecular "M" motif from the logo can inspire subtle background patterns or section dividers
- Professional but not sterile — warm touches through amber accents and human language
- Sections should flow naturally: dark → light → dark alternation creates visual rhythm

### Navigation

- Sticky/fixed top nav with logo and clear service links
- Mobile hamburger menu
- Smooth scroll for single-page, or clear page transitions for multi-page

### Imagery Style

- Abstract tech visuals, geometric patterns (complement the molecular logo)
- Photos of actual Tanzanian business settings where possible
- Icons: line-style, consistent weight, in brand colors
- Avoid generic stock photos of people pointing at screens

### Animations

- Subtle entrance animations on scroll (fade-in, slide-up)
- Smooth hover transitions on buttons and cards
- No flashy effects — professionalism over pizzazz
